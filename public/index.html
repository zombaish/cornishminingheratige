Cornish Mining Heritage Trust

The Industrial Frontier

Preserving the UNESCO World Heritage landscape and the legacy of Cornwall's pioneering era in global engineering.

The Copper Kingdom

During the 18th and 19th centuries, Cornwall’s engineering innovations transformed global mining operations, establishing the region as the undisputed capital of copper and tin extraction.

Global Influence

The landscape of Cornwall and West Devon underwent a radical transformation. The remains of engine houses and ports reflect a culture of extraordinary innovation that exported expertise worldwide.

Steam Innovation

Central to this industrial success was the high-pressure steam engine developed by local pioneers like Richard Trevithick, allowing miners to extract resources from unprecedented depths.
